Converging Canopy
Open-air social canopy pavilion
A lightweight, open-air social pavilion designed for informal gathering, quiet study, and casual interaction. The funnel-like forms create shaded micro-spaces for sitting, conversation, and individual use, while the central area supports small group exchange and temporary activities in a calm park setting.

07 — Process
From Soap Film to Canopy
Physical-to-digital translation methodology. Click each step to expand.
01Analog Frame Setup
A physical frame is prepared as the boundary condition for soap film testing. The frame defines the first spatial limits of the canopy.
02Soap Bubble Experiment
Soap film behavior is used to study curved surfaces, natural tension, and the early funnel-like canopy logic.
03Surface Reading
Photographs are analyzed to identify curvature, openings, edge behavior, and shaded spatial pockets.
04Digital Reconstruction
The form is reconstructed in Rhino and refined as a lightweight open-air pavilion with repeated funnel-like elements.
05Variation Development
Six options test scale, curve intensity, opening size, support rhythm, and the relation between individual and group spaces.
06Program Integration
Quiet study, informal gathering, conversation, sitting, and temporary activities are organized under the canopy system.
07Drawing Extraction
Plan and section are extracted from the final digital model and combined into the plan-section drawing sheet.
08Final Output
The project is presented through the GLB model, renders, Rhino views, diagrams, and technical drawings.
